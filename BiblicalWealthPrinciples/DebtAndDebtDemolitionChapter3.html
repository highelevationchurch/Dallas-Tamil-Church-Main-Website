Debt & Debt Demolition
 
Introduction
The biblical perspective of debt will help us to stay away from debt. If we have debt, we need to get out of it quickly. If we are out of debt, we need to stay out of it. In this lesson, we will study about the myths of debt and the biblical teachings on debt.
 
Debt and Demolition of Debt

4. Stay out of co-signing
  1. Co-signing is becoming common in our country. It tempts us to co-sign or legally become responsible to pay someone else's debt. The banks wants us to co-sign because they are not sure if the person borrowing is able to make the money to pay it off. The burden of paying will most likely fall on the co-signee. God wants us to stay out of co-signing.

    1. We should stay away from co-signing.

Snowball Method
The snowball method recommends paying off the smallest debt first, irrespective of the interest rate. It gains momentum, as you pay from the smallest to the biggest, not only in numbers but also the thrill of overcoming one debt at a time... For more details, please click on : http://www.daveramsey.com/blog/how-the-debt-snowball-method-works
It is time to start the snowball effect now!
 
Conclusion
Debt demolition requires supernatural intervention. May the Holy Spirit quicken us to get freedom from debt and to stay out of debt. Debt includes even home mortgages and car payments. We should use whatever resources that are available to pay off the debt. Even if it takes extra hours of work as the Lord commands, "Thou shalt work six days of the week", we should work hard and give preference to pay off the debt and then live on the rest of our income.
